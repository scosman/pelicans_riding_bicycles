# Governance

## Overview

The Pelicans Riding Bicycles Enthusiasts Club (PRBEC) operates under a lightweight, benevolent, migration-aware governance model. This document describes how decisions are made, how maintainers are appointed, and how the club remains accountable to the broader pelican-riding-bicycle community.

## Mission

PRBEC exists to curate, preserve, and extend the canonical corpus of SVG depictions of pelicans riding bicycles, in service of:

1. The aesthetic enrichment of the open-source commons.
2. The continuing dignity of pelicans.
3. The continuing dignity of bicycles.
4. Joy.

## Roles

### Contributor

Anyone who has opened a PR that has been merged. Contributors have no formal governance role but are thanked profusely and invited to all (zero) PRBEC events.

### Maintainer

Maintainers are responsible for reviewing PRs, triaging issues, and safeguarding the canonical pelican set. Maintainers are added by unanimous consent of existing maintainers, on the basis of sustained contribution and demonstrable alignment with PRBEC values.

### Chief Pelican Officer (CPO)

There is exactly one CPO at a time. The CPO is responsible for tie-breaking decisions, ceremonial duties, and representing PRBEC in external fora. The CPO serves until they either step down, migrate, or are respectfully asked by a supermajority of maintainers to step down.

### Honorary Members

PRBEC may, from time to time, extend honorary membership to individuals who have made distinguished contributions to the field of pelicans-riding-bicycles more broadly. Honorary members receive a warm feeling and, on request, a digital certificate rendered, naturally, in SVG.

## Decision Making

Decisions are made by rough consensus among maintainers. In the absence of consensus, the CPO decides. In the absence of the CPO, a coin flip is acceptable provided it is livestreamed.

**Types of decisions:**

| Decision | Process |
| --- | --- |
| Merging a PR that adds a pelican | One maintainer review |
| Merging a PR that removes a pelican | Two maintainer reviews |
| Merging a PR that modifies an existing pelican | Two maintainer reviews |
| Changing repository-wide conventions | Maintainer consensus |
| Changing the license | Unanimous maintainer consent |
| Changing this governance document | Unanimous maintainer consent |
| Adding a maintainer | Unanimous maintainer consent |
| Removing a maintainer for cause | Supermajority of remaining maintainers |
| Declaring an emergency | CPO prerogative |

## Transparency

All substantive decisions are made on the public repository via issues and PRs. There are no private mailing lists, private channels, or smoke-filled rooms. If a smoke-filled room ever becomes relevant, notes will be posted within 48 hours.

## Conflicts of Interest

Maintainers who have a material personal, professional, or romantic relationship to the author of a PR under review should disclose this and recuse themselves from the decision. "Material" is interpreted broadly. "Romantic relationship" is interpreted narrowly and is almost certainly not going to come up.
