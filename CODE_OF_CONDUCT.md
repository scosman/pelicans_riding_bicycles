# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as members, contributors, maintainers, curators, fellows, trustees, emeritus trustees, honorary pelicans, and corresponding members of the Pelicans Riding Bicycles Enthusiasts Club (PRBEC), pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free, beak-positive, wheel-affirming, saddle-inclusive, and pouch-safe experience for everyone, regardless of species (within reason), wingspan, gear ratio, preferred cadence, chain-lube orthodoxy, vector-graphics-authoring background, or opinion on the Oxford comma.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, pouch-forward, saddle-affirming, and migratory-schedule-aware community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other contributors, including those whose pelicans are, in your personal assessment, anatomically questionable.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and interpretations of what constitutes "riding" versus "merely being on" a bicycle.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback regarding beak geometry.
- Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, particularly mistakes involving incorrect pelican species classification.
- Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall pelican-riding-bicycle community and the pelicans themselves (even though the pelicans in question are vector illustrations and have no legal standing).

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, including but not limited to sexualized depictions of pelicans, bicycles, or pelican–bicycle interfaces.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment of any contributor or reviewer.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Asserting that ducks, geese, or swans are "basically pelicans." They are not. This is a zero-tolerance item.
- Submitting a pelican under false pretenses regarding its provenance, its authorship, or whether it was traced from a photograph.
- Gatekeeping about whether a given vehicle "really counts as a bicycle." The maintainers will gatekeep. That is our job. Thank you.
- Conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting, a semi-professional setting, a wetland, or a bike lane.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders (the PRBEC maintainers) are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, harmful, or off-brand.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, pull requests, SVG files, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate — though, candidly, in most cases the reason will be "this was not a pelican riding a bicycle."

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include using an official PRBEC e-mail address (there is no official PRBEC e-mail address), posting via an official social media account (there is no official social media account), or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event (no such events are scheduled, though we remain open to it).

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers via GitHub issues or direct message. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly, at a pace broadly consistent with pelican-circadian norms.

All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning from a community leader, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested. A pelican may or may not be mentioned.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence:** A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior or repeated submission of non-pelican waterfowl.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals or the entire order *Pelecaniformes*.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.
